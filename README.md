## Bank Marketing Campaign Analysis Using Python, SQL, and Excel.
This project explores key factors influencing customers' decisions to subscribe to a 
bank's term deposit. The analysis combines SQL for querying, Python for analysis and modeling,
and Excel for visualization.

## Key Insights
1. Customers aged 18-25 and those in management roles are more likely to subscribe.
   - Target young professionals and managerial groups in future campaigns.

2. Chi-square tests show both marital status and education significantly affect subscribtion rates.
   - Tailor marketing messages based on lifestyle and education segments.

3. May recorded the highest success rate, showing the importance of timing in campaign strategy.
   - Optimizing campaigns around periods with higher engagement.

4. Customers who previously responded positively show a higher chance of re-subscribing.
   - Focus on re-engaging previous positive respondents.

5. Clients without housing or personal loans are more likely to subscribe.
   - Consider financial freedom as a key segmentation factor.

6. Favorable economic conditions (low unemployment, stable prices) correlate with higher campaign success.
   - Align campaigns with positive economic trends.

7. Cellular contact (14.74%) performs far better than telephone (5.23%).
   - Prioritize modern communication channels for outreach.

8. Linear regression using the variable previous yielded:
   Subscription = 0.147 * Previous + 0.00827
   Past engagement is a good predictor of future subscriptions.


## Business Impact 
These insights can help the bank:
* Improve marketing targeting efficiency.
* Optimizing campaign timing and channel strategy.
* Reducing costs by focusing on high-potential customer groups.

## Conclusion
Strategic targetng and data-driven decisions can significantly boost campaign performance and 
customer engagement.
